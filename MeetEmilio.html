Clean Water

Access to clean drinking water is a human right and is critical to the overall health and long-term success of our communities. In the early 1990’s Emilio sued and won a lawsuit against Union Pacific Railroad to ensure the delivery of clean drinking water to rural residents in Kern County. As a Kern County Supervisor, Emilio will be an effective leader in promoting sensible water management solutions, conservation, and affordable clean drinking water.

Community Development

Emilio’s successful work with Kern County’s nonprofit agencies has improved the lives of working people. His expert knowledge of business law, real estate, and public financing has resulted in the creation of hundreds of new small businesses, construction of desperately needed housing and health clinics, and the establishment of credit unions. Emilio has been at the forefront of delivering affordable legal services throughout Kern County. He has served as Board Chair of the Self Help Federal Credit Union, Greater Bakersfield Legal Assistance, and was corporate counsel for Omni Family Health clinics.

View project

Homeless Crisis

Affordable and stable housing is key to building a strong and vibrant community. Emilio led efforts to construct almost 5,000 units of affordable housing across the Southwestern U.S. As our Supervisor, he will work together with state, county, and local service providers to bring new funding to help end the homeless crisis.
